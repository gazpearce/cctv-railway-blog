<p>In the ever-evolving landscape of security, maintaining your CCTV and alarm systems is not just a recommendation—it's a necessity. As we move into 2026, the importance of keeping these vital components working smoothly cannot be overstated. Regular maintenance ensures that your property remains secure, deterring potential threats and providing peace of mind for homeowners and business owners alike.</p>

<p>The consequences of neglecting such systems can be severe. A malfunctioning security system can leave a building vulnerable to break-ins, thefts, or even more serious incidents. In today's UK environment, where crime rates are always under scrutiny, the cost of such lapses can be astronomical—both in terms of financial loss and potential legal ramifications. It's not just about preventing theft; it's also about ensuring safety and compliance with regulatory standards.</p>

<p>When considering the cost of maintenance versus the cost of failure, the argument for regular upkeep becomes clear. Investing time and resources into maintaining your CCTV and alarm systems might seem like a significant expense in the short term. However, when compared to the potential financial, legal, and reputational fallout from a system failure, this preventative care is incredibly cost-effective. This guide aims to provide you with everything you need to know about securing your property through proper security system maintenance as we step into 2026.</p>

<hr class="faq-divider">

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<div class="faq-list">

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How often should CCTV be serviced?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

<p>CCTV systems should be professionally serviced annually. Monthly checks include: cleaning camera lenses with a microfiber cloth, checking cables for damage, and verifying recording is working. Quarterly checks include: reviewing camera angles (vegetation may have shifted), checking for spider webs blocking lenses, and testing night vision.</p>

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How much does CCTV maintenance cost?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

<p>Annual professional maintenance costs £50-£250 depending on system size. Basic health checks start at £65. Full system servicing with cleaning, testing, and firmware updates costs £100-£200. Commercial systems with many cameras range £200-£600 per year. Many installers offer maintenance plans that save 10-15% annually.</p>

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can I clean my CCTV cameras myself?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

<p>Yes. Use a microfiber cloth or lens cleaning wipe to gently clean the dome or lens. Do not use household cleaners or abrasive materials — they can damage the lens coating. Clean cameras monthly to prevent dust, cobwebs, and moisture from affecting image quality. Check IR LEDs are not blocked by debris.</p>

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How long do CCTV cameras last?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

<p>Quality CCTV cameras typically last 5-8 years. Dome cameras in sheltered positions last longer than bullet cameras exposed to weather. NVRs/DVRs last 5-7 years — hard drives may need replacing every 3-5 years. Rain, direct sunlight, and salt spray (coastal areas) significantly reduce lifespan.</p>

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What should I do when a camera stops working?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

<p>First: check power supply and cables, reboot the NVR/router, and check for firmware updates. If the camera is offline in the app but recording locally, the network connection may be the issue. If physically damaged, the camera unit likely needs replacement. Contact your installer for diagnosis.</p>

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Do smart security systems need maintenance?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

<p>Yes. Smart systems need: regular firmware updates (check for these monthly), battery replacement in wireless sensors (every 1-2 years), WiFi network health checks, and app updates. Smart doorbells need periodic cleaning of the button and camera lens. Cloud storage subscriptions should be reviewed annually.</p>

</div>
</details>

</div>
