Complete CCTV compliance guide for UK petrol stations and garages: forecourt coverage, number plate recognition, drive-off prevention, and payment area security.

<hr class="faq-divider">

## Frequently Asked Questions

<div class="faq-list">

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Is CCTV required at petrol stations?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

While not legally mandatory, CCTV is considered essential for petrol station security. The Petroleum Enforcement Authorities (PEA) and major fuel brands strongly recommend comprehensive CCTV coverage. In practice, every UK petrol station operates CCTV to deter drive-offs, monitor fuel deliveries, protect staff, and provide evidence for insurance claims.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What areas need camera coverage at a petrol station?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Essential coverage includes: all fuel pumps and forecourt areas (capturing vehicle registration plates and driver identification), the payment kiosk or shop interior (till areas and entry/exit), cash handling areas, fuel delivery points (for supply security), the car wash and air/water stations, and all entrances and exits to the site. ANPR cameras at entry and exit points are strongly recommended.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can petrol stations use facial recognition?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

The use of facial recognition technology in petrol stations is subject to strict UK GDPR requirements. It processes special category biometric data under Article 9, requiring a separate lawful condition and additional safeguards. Most petrol stations use standard CCTV and ANPR rather than facial recognition. If facial recognition is deployed, a DPIA is mandatory, and the use must be notified to the ICO.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How long should garage CCTV footage be kept?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Standard retention is 30 days for routine CCTV. However, ANPR data is often retained for longer — typically up to 90 days — because drive-off and fuel theft investigations may take time to be reported. The retention period must be documented in the station's CCTV policy, with older data automatically deleted. Footage related to specific incidents should be preserved until the matter is resolved.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What signage is needed for petrol station CCTV?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Petrol stations must display clear signage at all entry points stating that CCTV and ANPR are in operation. Signs should include: the purpose of surveillance, the data controller's identity (the station operator), contact details, and information about rights of access. Additional signage is recommended at the payment kiosk and fuel pumps. The ICO provides template signage on their website.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Do petrol stations need ICO registration for CCTV?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Yes. All petrol stations and garages using CCTV that captures identifiable individuals must pay the ICO data protection fee. This applies regardless of whether the station is a large brand or an independent operator. The fee for small businesses is £52 or £78 per year (reduced to £47 or £73 by direct debit).

</div>
</details>
