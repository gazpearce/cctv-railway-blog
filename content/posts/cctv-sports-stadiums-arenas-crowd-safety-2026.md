Large-scale CCTV deployment for UK sports stadiums and arenas: SGSA compliance, crowd monitoring, entry control, concourse coverage, and safety certificate requirements.

<hr class="faq-divider">

## Frequently Asked Questions

<div class="faq-list">

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Is CCTV mandatory in UK sports stadiums?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Yes, effectively. The Sports Grounds Safety Authority (SGSA) requires sports grounds with a capacity of 5,000 or more to have CCTV as part of their safety certificate requirements. The Surveillance Camera Code of Practice and the SGSA's Guide to Safety at Sports Grounds (the 'Green Guide') both specify minimum CCTV coverage for spectator safety and crowd management.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What areas must stadium CCTV cover?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Comprehensive coverage must include: all entry turnstiles and gates, concourses and circulation areas, seating stands and terraces (to monitor crowd behaviour and safety), pitch/perimeter areas, car parks and approach routes, and all exit points. Modern stadiums operate hundreds of cameras linked to a central control room with dedicated security personnel monitoring live feeds.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What resolution is needed for stadium CCTV?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Stadiums require high-resolution cameras — typically 4K (8MP) or higher — to clearly identify individuals across large distances. PTZ (pan-tilt-zoom) cameras are essential for following incidents in real time. The SGSA recommends cameras with sufficient resolution to read shirt numbers and identify facial features from the furthest point in each zone.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How long do stadiums keep CCTV footage?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Standard retention for stadium CCTV is 30 days, consistent with ICO guidance. However, for major events or matches where incidents are more likely, footage may be retained for longer — typically until any post-event investigation is complete. Premier League clubs often retain match-day footage for the full season for safety and disciplinary purposes, documented in their CCTV policy.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Do stadiums need CCTV signage?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Yes. Sports grounds must display prominent CCTV signage at all public entrances. Signs should inform spectators that CCTV is in operation for their safety and security. Many stadiums also include a statement about CCTV use in their ticketing terms and conditions, which provides an additional layer of transparency under UK GDPR.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can stadium CCTV be used for disciplinary purposes?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Yes, within limits. Stadium CCTV footage is commonly used to identify spectators involved in disorder, pitch invasions, or breach of ground regulations. However, using CCTV for routine staff monitoring in non-public areas requires separate justification and a documented DPIA. The ICO has published specific guidance on CCTV for staff monitoring in workplace settings.

</div>
</details>

</div>
