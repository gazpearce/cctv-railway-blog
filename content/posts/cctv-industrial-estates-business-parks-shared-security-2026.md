CCTV strategies for industrial estates and business parks: shared perimeter security, unit-level access control, landlord responsibilities, and tenant data protection considerations.

<hr class="faq-divider">

## Frequently Asked Questions

<div class="faq-list">

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Who is responsible for CCTV on an industrial estate?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Responsibility depends on the CCTV ownership. The landlord or management company is typically responsible for shared areas — perimeter fencing, main entrances, estate roads, and communal car parks. Individual tenants are responsible for CCTV within their own units. The legal data controller is whoever determines the purpose and means of processing — for shared cameras, this is the estate management company.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What areas should industrial estate CCTV cover?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Estate-wide CCTV should cover: the main entrance and any secondary vehicle gates (with ANPR), pedestrian access points, estate roads and circulation areas, communal car parks and loading bays, bin storage and service areas, and perimeter fencing and boundaries. Individual units should add their own CCTV for: unit entrances (both personnel and roller shutter doors), internal warehouse or production areas, and office entrances.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can estate-wide CCTV be used for tenant disputes?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Estate CCTV recorded in shared areas is jointly managed. Tenants cannot demand footage of other tenants without a legitimate purpose. Under UK GDPR, any tenant can submit a subject access request for footage they appear in, but the management company must balance this against the data protection rights of other individuals captured. A clear, documented policy on CCTV access should be established.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How should industrial estate CCTV be signposted?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Signage must be displayed at all estate entrances stating that CCTV is in operation for security purposes. Signs should identify the data controller (the estate management company) and provide contact details. Individual tenants should also display their own CCTV signage at their unit entrances if they operate separate systems. This ensures visitors and delivery drivers are aware of surveillance.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>How long should industrial estate CCTV footage be kept?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Standard retention is 30 days. However, for large industrial estates where serious incidents (theft, vandalism, trespass) may not be reported immediately, a retention period of 60 days may be justifiable if documented in the CCTV policy. Footage related to specific incidents should be preserved until the investigation or insurance claim is resolved.

</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Do industrial estate tenants need their own CCTV?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">

Shared estate CCTV covers communal areas but not individual units. Tenants responsible for valuable stock, equipment, or vehicles should install their own CCTV covering: unit entrances and loading bays, internal storage and production areas, and staff-only areas. Tenant CCTV must be separately registered with the ICO if it captures identifiable individuals.

</div>
</details>
