CCTV for Airports and Transport Hubs — National Security Standards UK 2026

Comprehensive guide to CCTV at UK airports and transport hubs: DfT compliance, terminal coverage, baggage hall security, car park surveillance, and counter-terrorism requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CCTV mandatory in UK airports?

Yes. Under the Department for Transport’s aviation security requirements and the National Aviation Security Programme (NASP), all UK airports must operate comprehensive CCTV systems. This is a legal requirement for aviation security, counter-terrorism, and public safety. Airports are also subject to the Surveillance Camera Code of Practice and regular inspection by the Civil Aviation Authority.

What are the key areas for airport CCTV coverage?

Airports require extensive CCTV coverage including: all terminal entrances and exits, check-in areas and departure halls, security search areas (with dedicated high-resolution cameras), baggage reclaim halls, airside departure gates and boarding areas, baggage handling and sorting areas, car parks and drop-off zones, and all perimeter fencing and access points. Major airports operate thousands of cameras.

How long do airports retain CCTV footage?

Airport CCTV retention varies by zone and purpose. Standard terminal CCTV is typically retained for 30 days. Security search area footage may be retained for longer — up to 90 days — for audit and compliance purposes. Footage related to security incidents or police investigations is preserved until the matter is resolved. All retention periods must be documented in the airport’s CCTV policy.

What CCTV rules apply to airport car parks?

Airport car parks must have comprehensive CCTV and ANPR coverage at entry/exit points, pedestrian walkways, and throughout the parking areas. The ICO’s code of practice requires clear signage informing drivers that CCTV and ANPR are in operation. Retention of car park ANPR data is typically 30 days unless needed for a specific investigation.

Do transport hubs need CCTV signage?

Yes. All transport hubs must display CCTV signage at all public entrances, in car parks, and at key circulation points. The Department for Transport recommends that signage includes the purpose of surveillance, the operating authority, and contact details. Given the large number of cameras in transport hubs, directional signage (‘This area is covered by CCTV’) is also recommended.

Can the public access airport CCTV footage?

Yes, under UK GDPR Article 15, individuals can make a subject access request for footage they appear in. However, airports may restrict access where disclosure would affect national security, crime prevention, or the rights of others. Requests should be directed to the airport’s data protection officer. Transport hubs should have a documented SAR process that balances individual rights with security considerations.
