CCTV for Holiday Parks and Campsites — Seasonal Security UK 2026

How to implement effective CCTV for holiday parks, caravan sites, and campsites: entrance control, seasonal considerations, static caravan security, and guest privacy protection.

By Gary Pearce May 18, 2026 3 min read

Frequently Asked Questions

Should holiday parks have CCTV?

CCTV is increasingly common in UK holiday parks for good reason. It protects guest vehicles and caravans, deters anti-social behaviour, monitors entrance and exit points, and provides evidence in the event of incidents. However, parks must carefully balance security with the holiday atmosphere — guests should feel safe, not watched. Discreet dome cameras are preferred over more obvious bullet cameras.

Where should campsite cameras be placed?

Recommended locations include: the main entrance and reception area (with ANPR for vehicle access), touring field entry points, storage compounds for static caravans and boats, the shop, bar, restaurant and entertainment areas, and car parks. Cameras should NOT be placed in toilet and shower blocks, near swimming pools (for safeguarding reasons), or pointing directly at individual touring pitches or static caravan windows.

Do holiday parks need CCTV signage?

Yes. Under UK GDPR, holiday parks must display clear CCTV signage at the main entrance, reception, and any monitored facilities. Signs should include: the purpose of surveillance, the data controller’s identity (park operator), contact details, and information about subject access rights. Seasonal parks should review their signage annually as signs may need replacing after winter weather.

What are the rules for seasonal CCTV decommissioning?

Holiday parks that close during winter months should have a documented process for CCTV decommissioning. Recordings should be securely deleted rather than simply left on powered-down systems. If cameras are required for winter storage security, a separate CCTV policy for the closed season should be documented. Equipment should be inspected for weather damage before each season.

How long should holiday park CCTV be retained?

Standard retention is 30 days. However, for holiday parks with static caravans that may not be visited for weeks, a retention period of 60 days may be justifiable if documented in the CCTV policy. Footage related to incidents should be preserved until the investigation or insurance claim is resolved. Automatic overwrite should be enabled for routine footage.

Can guests request CCTV footage from holiday parks?

Yes. Any guest captured on CCTV can make a subject access request under UK GDPR. The park operator must respond within one calendar month. Given that holiday parks may have hundreds of cameras covering large sites, the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025’s ‘stop the clock’ provision is particularly useful — parks can ask guests to specify dates, times, and locations to locate relevant footage.
