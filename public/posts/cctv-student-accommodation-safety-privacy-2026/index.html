CCTV for Student Accommodation — Safety and Privacy Guide UK 2026

Essential guidance for PBSA providers and university halls: CCTV in communal areas, ANUK/Unipol compliance, resident privacy rights, and best practice for student safety.

By Gary Pearce May 18, 2026 3 min read

Frequently Asked Questions

Can student accommodation have CCTV?

Yes, CCTV is standard in purpose-built student accommodation (PBSA) and university halls of residence. It provides essential security for residents and protects property. However, CCTV must be limited to communal areas only — entrances, corridors, common rooms, laundry rooms, and external perimeters. Individual bedrooms, bathrooms, and kitchen areas within flats must not have CCTV.

What are ANUK/Unipol requirements for student CCTV?

ANUK and Unipol are the main accreditation bodies for student accommodation in the UK. Their codes of practice require that accommodation providers have appropriate security measures, which may include CCTV. Accredited providers must have a clear CCTV policy, display signage, and ensure residents are informed about surveillance at the point of signing their tenancy agreement.

Can students opt out of being recorded by CCTV?

Students cannot opt out of CCTV in communal areas where it is necessary for security purposes. However, they have the right to object under UK GDPR Article 21, and the accommodation provider must consider the objection. If the provider has a compelling legitimate interest for the CCTV (such as safeguarding), they may continue processing. Students always retain the right to request footage they appear in.

How should student accommodation CCTV be signposted?

Clear signage must be displayed at all building entrances, at the entrance to each floor or corridor section, and in any monitored communal areas. Signs must include: the identity of the data controller (university or private provider), the purpose of CCTV, contact information for privacy queries, and details of how to make a subject access request.

How long is student CCTV footage kept?

The standard retention period is 30 days, after which footage is automatically overwritten. If an incident occurs involving a resident, footage should be preserved until the investigation is concluded. Student accommodation providers must document their retention policy and ensure secure storage with access limited to authorised security and management personnel.

Do private student housing providers need ICO registration?

Yes. Any provider using CCTV that captures identifiable individuals must register with the ICO and pay the data protection fee. This applies to universities, private PBSA operators, and letting agents managing student properties. The fee depends on organisation size, starting at £52 per year for small providers.
