CCTV for Marinas and Boat Storage — Marine Security UK 2026

Complete guide to securing marinas and boat storage facilities with CCTV: coastal weatherproof cameras, pontoon coverage, vessel protection, and 24/7 remote monitoring solutions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of CCTV cameras work in coastal environments?

Coastal and marina environments require specialist cameras with IP66 or IP67 weatherproof ratings. Corrosion-resistant materials such as marine-grade stainless steel or coated aluminium housings are essential. Dome cameras with internal heaters and wipers are recommended to handle salt spray, condensation, and adverse weather. Look for cameras specifically rated for marine environments.

Where should marina cameras be positioned?

Key camera locations include: marina entrance/exit gates (with ANPR for vehicle access), the main gate and pedestrian access points, fuel dock and pump-out stations, boat yard and dry storage areas, car parks (for vehicle and trailer security), and office/marina building entrances. Cameras on entrance pontoons can provide vessel movement monitoring.

Can marina CCTV cover moored boats?

Yes, but cameras should cover communal access points and walkways rather than being aimed directly into individual boat cabins or cockpits, where there is a reasonable expectation of privacy. Wide-angle cameras covering the general pontoon area are acceptable. The ICO recommends fixed cameras rather than PTZ in marina settings to avoid disproportionate surveillance.

What are the power considerations for marina CCTV?

Marina CCTV installations often require careful power planning. Options include: power over Ethernet (PoE) for cameras near buildings, solar-powered wireless cameras for remote pontoons, and 4G-connected cameras where cabling is impractical. Battery-backed systems are recommended to ensure continuous recording during power outages. All electrical work should be carried out by a qualified marine electrician.

How long should marina CCTV footage be retained?

Standard retention is 30 days for marina CCTV. Given that boat theft and marina damage may not be discovered immediately (boats may be visited infrequently), some marinas retain footage for 60-90 days. This extended retention should be documented and justified in the marina’s CCTV policy. Footage related to specific incidents should be preserved until the matter is resolved.

Do marinas need CCTV signage?

Yes. Marinas must display CCTV signage at all entrances: the main gate, pedestrian access, the fuel dock, and the marina office. Signs should inform boat owners and visitors that CCTV and ANPR are in operation, identify the data controller (marina operator), and provide contact information for privacy-related queries. The Surveillance Camera Code of Practice recommends additional signage at key monitored locations.
