CCTV for Universities and Higher Education — Campus Security Guide UK 2026

How UK universities can implement effective campus-wide CCTV: student safety, accommodation monitoring, lecture hall security, and compliance with education sector data protection rules.

By Gary Pearce May 18, 2026 3 min read

Frequently Asked Questions

Can universities put CCTV in student accommodation?

Yes, but only in communal areas such as entrances, corridors, laundries, and common rooms. CCTV must NOT be installed inside individual student rooms or bathrooms. The ICO expects universities to complete a DPIA before installing accommodation CCTV, limit retention to 30 days, and display clear signage at all accommodation entrances.

Are lecture halls monitored by CCTV?

Some universities have CCTV in lecture theatres and large teaching spaces, primarily to protect expensive AV equipment and for fire safety monitoring. However, small seminar rooms and tutorial spaces typically do not have CCTV due to the higher expectation of privacy in smaller-group teaching. Any CCTV in teaching spaces must be clearly signposted.

What are the rules for campus perimeter CCTV?

Campus boundary and entrance CCTV is standard practice for UK universities. Cameras should cover: all pedestrian and vehicle entrances, car parks, bicycle storage areas, and key walkways. ANPR cameras at vehicle entrances help manage parking and security. Universities must ensure perimeter cameras are not pointed into neighbouring residential properties.

Do universities need to register their CCTV with the ICO?

Yes. Universities are data controllers for their CCTV systems and must pay the ICO data protection fee. Higher education institutions processing large-scale surveillance data are also required to appoint a Data Protection Officer. Many universities publish their CCTV policies online, detailing camera locations, retention periods, and access procedures.

How should university libraries handle CCTV?

University library CCTV should cover entrances/exits and high-value collections areas. CCTV should not be positioned to capture individual study desks or computer screens, as this would be disproportionate. The Surveillance Camera Code of Practice recommends that library CCTV policies be developed in consultation with student representatives to address privacy concerns.

Can students request their university CCTV footage?

Yes. Students have the same right of access under UK GDPR Article 15 as any other data subject. They can request footage they appear in by submitting a subject access request to the university’s data protection officer. The university must respond within one calendar month. The Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 provides for a ‘stop the clock’ provision if additional information is needed to locate specific footage.
