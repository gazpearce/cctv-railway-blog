CCTV for Hospitals and NHS Facilities — Compliance and Security Guide 2026

How NHS trusts and private hospitals can deploy CCTV lawfully: patient privacy protections, A&E security, car park surveillance, and compliance with UK GDPR and the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025.

By Gary Pearce May 18, 2026 3 min read

Frequently Asked Questions

Do hospitals need CCTV?

Yes, CCTV is essential for hospital security, protecting patients, staff, visitors, and assets. However, healthcare settings require careful balancing of security needs against patient privacy rights. The ICO expects NHS trusts to complete a Data Protection Impact Assessment before deploying or expanding CCTV, document their lawful basis (usually public task or legitimate interests), and implement strict access controls.

Can hospitals install CCTV in patient rooms?

Patient bedrooms and treatment areas have a high expectation of privacy. CCTV in these areas is only permitted in exceptional circumstances — for example, in psychiatric intensive care units where patient safety is at immediate risk, and even then only with documented justification, a completed DPIA, clear signage, and strict access limitations. Routine CCTV in general ward areas is not recommended.

What are the rules for A&E department CCTV?

A&E departments commonly use CCTV for security purposes — monitoring waiting rooms, entrances, and corridors. Specific rules include: cameras must not point into treatment cubicles or consultation rooms, clear signage must be displayed at all entrances stating CCTV is in operation with contact details for the data controller, retention should be limited to 30 days, and audio recording is not recommended due to patient confidentiality.

Do NHS trusts need to register with the ICO for CCTV?

Yes. All NHS trusts and private hospitals using CCTV that captures identifiable individuals must pay the ICO data protection fee. The fee tier depends on the organisation size, ranging from £52 to £2,900 per year. Trusts must also appoint a Data Protection Officer if processing large-scale CCTV data.

How should hospital car park CCTV be managed?

Hospital car park CCTV should be positioned to cover vehicle entry/exit points and pedestrian walkways, not pointed at vehicles in a way that captures passengers. ANPR (Automatic Number Plate Recognition) systems for car park management must comply with UK GDPR and the Surveillance Camera Code of Practice. Signage must inform drivers and pedestrians that CCTV and ANPR are in operation.

Can the police request hospital CCTV footage?

Yes. Under the Criminal Procedures and Investigations Act 1996, police can request CCTV footage relevant to an investigation. NHS trusts should have a documented procedure for handling police requests, maintain a log of all footage disclosures, and ensure footage is preserved without alteration when requested.
