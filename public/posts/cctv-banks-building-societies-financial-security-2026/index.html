CCTV for Banks and Building Societies — Financial Security Standards UK 2026

Comprehensive guide to CCTV standards for UK banks and building societies: counter surveillance, ATM monitoring, vault protection, and FCA compliance requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CCTV mandatory in UK banks?

While there is no single law that explicitly mandates CCTV in banks, the Financial Conduct Authority’s Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR) effectively requires firms to have appropriate security measures. The British Banking Association’s guidelines strongly recommend CCTV coverage at all customer-facing areas, cash points, and entry/exit points. In practice, every UK bank branch has CCTV.

What areas must bank CCTV cover?

Critical coverage areas include: all customer entrances and exits (both external and internal), counter/service positions (to capture face-to-face transactions), ATM lobbies and external cash points (including ANPR for drive-through ATMs), cash handling rooms and vaults, and staff-only areas. Each camera should be positioned to capture clear facial images of all individuals entering secure areas.

How long do banks keep CCTV footage?

Banks typically retain CCTV footage for 31 to 90 days, longer than the standard 30-day recommendation for most businesses. This extended retention is justified by the need to investigate financial fraud, which may not be reported immediately. Banks must document their retention justification in their CCTV policy and ensure footage is securely stored with encryption and strict access controls.

Can bank CCTV be used in criminal prosecutions?

Yes, bank CCTV footage is routinely used as evidence in criminal prosecutions for robbery, fraud, and other financial crimes. To be admissible, the footage must be handled in accordance with the Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE) codes of practice. Banks should maintain a clear chain of custody for any footage provided to law enforcement.

What are the rules for ATM CCTV?

ATM CCTV must capture clear facial images of all users while minimising capture of PIN entry. Cameras should be positioned above or beside the ATM, angled downward to capture the user’s face while avoiding the keypad area. Modern ATMs have integrated cameras that comply with these requirements. Signage must be displayed at each ATM location informing users that recording is taking place.

Do building societies need CCTV data protection registration?

Yes. All financial institutions using CCTV that captures identifiable individuals must pay the ICO data protection fee. Banks and building societies processing large-scale CCTV data are also required to appoint a Data Protection Officer under UK GDPR Article 37. Failure to register can result in fines of up to £17.5 million or 4% of global turnover.
